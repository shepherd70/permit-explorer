About How this works & where the data comes from

Live, not stored

Every chart is built on the spot by querying the City of Calgary's open-data API the moment you open this page. Nothing is cached or hand-entered — change a filter and the questions are re-run against the live dataset.

City view vs. detail view

With a broad filter (hundreds of thousands of permits) the tool shows city totals computed by the server. Narrow things down below ~30,000 permits — one community, or a single year city-wide — and it automatically switches to detail view: individual permits on the map, the records table, median costs, and renovation-lifecycle analysis.

What the numbers mean

  • Estimated cost is the applicant's projected construction value — not final or assessed value. Some permits have no cost on file.
  • Days to issue = issued date − applied date.
  • Completion rate = completed ÷ resolved permits (excludes ones still in review or on hold).
  • Renovation lifecycle pairs a new build with the first later non-demolition permit at the same address.
  • Map bubbles use the average location of permits in each community.

Source

City of Calgary Open Data — Building Permits (c2es-76ed). Independent project, not affiliated with The City of Calgary.
